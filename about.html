Course Context

ENG-203 Engineering Materials

This project was completed as part of ENG-203 at the Engineering Department. The course focuses on engineering design, materials selection, and lean manufacturing principles applied to real-world automation challenges.

The gripper project was chosen to bridge mechanical design, material science, and manufacturing process knowledge within a single integrated deliverable.

Project Scope

What We Built

A modular two-finger gripper designed for lean automation. The modularity allows finger profiles to be swapped without dismantling the actuator assembly, reducing changeover time in multi-variant production lines.

Three materials — Aluminum 6061-T6, CFRP, and SLS PA12 Nylon — were evaluated through FEA simulation and physical load testing to identify the optimal candidate for each use-case scenario.
